Word of the Day

rival

/ˈraɪvəl/
verb / noun / adjective
📖 Meaning

to compete with someone or something that is strong or successful

🧠 Usage Explanation

Use "rival" when describing competition between products, companies, or AI models. It is common in tech news.

🧱 Sentence Pattern
  • rival a leading product
  • a rival company
  • rival AI models
💬 Examples
Moonshot AI says Kimi K3 can rival leading AI models.
AI news
Alibaba Cloud has to explain its strengths against rival platforms.
cloud business
Customers often compare rival solutions before making a decision.
sales meeting
A smaller vendor can still rival larger players in a niche market.
OPC customer
The model rivals others in reasoning, but we still need security testing.
model evaluation
🧩 Common Collocations
rival leading models
The startup claims its model can rival leading models.
rival platform
The client is also evaluating a rival platform.
rival solution
We need to compare our proposal with rival solutions.
