Word of the Day

leverage

/ˈlev.ər.ɪdʒ/ /ˈliː.vər.ɪdʒ/
verb · also noun
📖 Definition

to use something to maximum advantage

To strategically use a resource, asset, or opportunity to achieve a better outcome — not just "using" it, but using it smartly.

💬 Examples
"We can leverage our existing cloud infrastructure to reduce costs."
— at work, talking about using what you already have
"She leveraged her network to find a new job opportunity."
— everyday life, using connections smartly
"How can we leverage AI to drive better business outcomes?"
— a question you might ask at work
"The startup leveraged customer data to personalize their product."
— tech/business context
🔗 Synonyms & Antonyms
utilize harness capitalize on exploit make the most of
waste misuse neglect
🧩 Common Collocations
leverage + [something]
leverage data / leverage technology / leverage relationships
leverage + [something] + to + [verb]
leverage AI to automate processes / leverage our platform to scale
have / gain leverage (noun form)
"They gained leverage in the negotiation."
