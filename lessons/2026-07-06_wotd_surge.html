surge

/sɜːrdʒ/
noun · verb
📖 Meaning

a sudden powerful increase, rise, or forward movement
As a noun: a sudden increase or rush
As a verb: to rise or increase suddenly and strongly

🧠 Usage Explanation

Surge is used when something increases quickly — demand, prices, traffic, emotions, even electricity. In cloud computing, a "traffic surge" means a sudden jump in user requests. In healthcare, "patient surge" means a sudden increase in patients. It is stronger than "increase" and implies speed and pressure.

💬 Examples
The company experienced a sudden surge in user traffic after the product launch.
cloud scenario
Cloud platforms can automatically handle traffic surges during peak hours.
elastic cloud
There has been a surge in demand for AI-powered healthcare solutions.

finance
