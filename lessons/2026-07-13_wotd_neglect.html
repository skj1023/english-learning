neglect

/nɪˈɡlekt/
verb / noun
📖 Meaning

to fail to give enough care or attention to something or someone

🧠 Usage Explanation

Use “neglect” when an important duty, risk, or detail is ignored. It is stronger and more formal than “forget.”

🧱 Sentence Pattern
  • neglect something
  • neglect to do something
  • do not neglect the importance of something
💬 Examples
If you neglect compliance, you expose the client to regulatory risk.
healthcare cloud
Many startups neglect security in the early stages.
startup
We should not neglect user education after deployment.
client success
The team neglected to document the access control policy.
project risk
Neglecting customer feedback can damage long-term trust.
business
🧩 Common Collocations
neglect compliance
Never neglect compliance in healthcare projects.
neglect security
Some teams neglect security when they move too fast.
neglect to do something
They neglected to update the audit trail.
