Word of the Day

neglect

/nɪˈɡlekt/
verb · also noun
📖 Definition

to fail to give proper care, attention, or time to someone or something

Unlike "ignore" (which is intentional), neglect often carries a sense of unintentional or gradual failing — you know you should do it, but you don't.

💬 Examples
"We've been so busy that we neglected to update the documentation."
— at work, something slipped through the cracks
"Don't neglect your health while chasing deadlines."
— everyday advice, work-life balance
"The project failed because the team neglected user feedback during development."
— business/product context
"He neglected to mention that the deadline had already passed."
— conveniently forgot to share important info
🔗 Synonyms & Antonyms
ignore disregard overlook omit skip
care for attend to prioritize maintain
🧩 Common Collocations
neglect + [noun]
neglect your health / neglect your duties / neglect customer feedback
neglect to + [verb]
neglect to mention / neglect to update / neglect to inform
in a state of neglect (noun form)
"The garden fell into a state of neglect."
