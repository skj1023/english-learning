Word of the Day

predictability

/prɪˌdɪktəˈbɪləti/
noun
📖 Meaning

the quality of being easy to estimate, plan, or forecast

🧠 Usage Explanation

Use "predictability" when discussing cost, performance, risk, or timelines with customers. It is especially useful for OPC and healthcare clients.

🧱 Sentence Pattern
  • cost predictability
  • improve predictability
  • lack of predictability
💬 Examples
OPC customers often care about cost predictability.
budget discussion
Reserved capacity can improve cost predictability.
cloud pricing
Healthcare systems need performance predictability during peak hours.
healthcare workload
The phased plan gives the customer more timeline predictability.
project planning
A lack of predictability makes stakeholders nervous.
business communication
