poll

/poʊl/
noun
📖 Meaning

a survey of public opinion; the process of asking people what they think about a topic and recording the results
Often used in politics, market research, and customer surveys.

🧠 Usage Explanation

A "poll" is not the same as a "questionnaire" — a poll usually asks a very specific question to measure opinion (e.g., "Do you support this policy?"), while a questionnaire may gather more detailed data. In business, "poll" can also be used informally: "I did a quick poll among the team" means you asked everyone's opinion quickly.

💬 Examples
A recent poll showed that 70% of healthcare leaders believe AI will transform the industry.
healthcare AI
We conducted an internal poll to understand which cloud features clients value most.
business
The opinion poll asked voters which policy they prioritized for the next election.
politics
I did a quick poll among my team about which platform to use.

Customer satisfaction polls help us improve our product.
