career-oriented

/kəˈrɪr ˌɔːriəntɪd/
adjective
📖 Meaning

focused on practical skills and future career outcomes

🧠 Usage Explanation

Use this to describe education, assignments, training, or decisions that are closely connected to future work. It is more precise than “practical.”

🧱 Sentence Pattern
  • career-oriented education
  • a career-oriented program
  • more career-oriented than textbook-based
💬 Examples
The assignments in Australia were more career-oriented.
education comparison
I prefer career-oriented learning because it helps me apply knowledge at work.
personal preference
A career-oriented course should include real business cases.
course design
Compared with my Chinese university experience, my master’s program felt more career-oriented.
study abroad
For adults, career-oriented learning is usually easier to stay motivated for.
learning habit
🧩 Common Collocations
career-oriented education
Career-oriented education connects learning with future work.
career-oriented program
The program is designed for working professionals.
career-oriented training
Career-oriented training focuses on practical skills.
